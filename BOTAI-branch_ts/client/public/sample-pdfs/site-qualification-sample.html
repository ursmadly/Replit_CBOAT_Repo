Site Qualification Report

Protocol: PRO001

Site ID: 123

Date of Visit: November 5, 2024

Executive Summary

Site 123 (Diabetes Research Center, Philadelphia) was evaluated for participation in Protocol PRO001. Overall, the site demonstrates strong capabilities to conduct the study with adequate facilities, experienced staff, and access to the target patient population.

Recommendation: Approved for study participation with an initial enrollment target of 20 patients.

3. Patient Population

The site has access to a substantial patient population matching the study criteria. Dr. Williams reported seeing approximately 250 patients with type 2 diabetes per month in the clinic.

3.1 Recruitment Capabilities

  • Electronic medical records system with search capability for identifying eligible patients
  • Database of approximately 1,800 type 2 diabetes patients
  • Established referral network with 5 local primary care practices
  • Previous successful recruitment strategy including social media and local newspaper advertisements
  • Historical enrollment rate of 3-5 patients per month for similar studies

3.2 Competing Studies

The site is currently participating in two other diabetes clinical trials:

  • Study ABC-123: Investigating a SGLT-2 inhibitor (enrollment complete, in follow-up phase)
  • Study XYZ-789: Investigating a DPP-4 inhibitor (different patient population, minimal overlap expected)

Dr. Williams confirmed that these studies will not compete significantly with the PRO001 trial, as they target different patient populations or are in later stages.

4.3 Laboratory Facilities

The site has an on-site CLIA-certified laboratory capable of performing all protocol-required local laboratory tests. The laboratory participates in external quality control programs and maintains appropriate certifications.

5. Standard Operating Procedures

The site maintains comprehensive SOPs covering all aspects of clinical trial conduct, including:

  • Informed consent process
  • Source documentation
  • Adverse event reporting
  • Drug accountability
  • Sample handling and processing
  • Data entry and query resolution
  • Essential document maintenance

All SOPs were reviewed and found to be current, with the last revision within the past 12 months.

6. Quality Assurance

The site has established quality control procedures including:

  • Regular internal audits of study documentation
  • Weekly team meetings to review study progress and address issues
  • Training program for all staff involved in clinical research
  • Process for handling protocol deviations and implementing corrective actions

The site underwent an FDA inspection in May 2024 with no significant findings.

7. Areas for Improvement

While the site is well-qualified overall, the following areas could be strengthened:

  • Additional training on the specific requirements for HbA1c sample processing is recommended
  • Consider designating a backup study coordinator to ensure coverage during planned absences
  • Update the site's participant retention strategy to address potential challenges with the 24-week follow-up period

8. Conclusion and Recommendations

Site 123 meets all requirements to participate in Protocol PRO001. The investigator and staff are experienced, the facilities are appropriate, and the site has access to a suitable patient population.

Specific recommendations:

  • Approve site with initial enrollment target of 20 patients
  • Provide additional training on HbA1c sample processing during site initiation visit
  • Schedule first monitoring visit within 2 weeks of first patient enrollment
  • Assist site in developing enhanced retention strategies for 24-week follow-up
