Clinical Trial Protocol PRO001

Protocol Number: PRO001

Version: 2.1

Date: January 15, 2025

1. Protocol Summary

This clinical trial protocol outlines the procedures and methodologies for assessing the efficacy and safety of the investigational product in patients with Type 2 Diabetes.

2. Study Objectives and Endpoints

2.1 Primary Objective

To evaluate the efficacy of the investigational product in reducing HbA1c levels compared to placebo in adults with Type 2 Diabetes.

2.2 Secondary Objectives

  • To assess changes in fasting plasma glucose levels
  • To evaluate changes in body weight
  • To assess insulin sensitivity and beta-cell function

2.3 Primary Endpoint

Change from baseline in HbA1c at Week 24.

2.4 Secondary Endpoints

  • Change from baseline in fasting plasma glucose at Week 24
  • Change from baseline in body weight at Week 24
  • Proportion of patients achieving HbA1c <7.0% at Week 24

3. Study Design and Procedures

This is a Phase III, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, parallel-group, multicenter study in adult patients with Type 2 Diabetes.

3.1 Study Duration

The total duration of the study is approximately 30 weeks, consisting of a 4-week screening period, a 24-week treatment period, and a 2-week follow-up period.

3.2 Study Visits

Study visits will occur at Screening, Baseline (Day 1), Weeks 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 24, and Follow-up (Week 26).

4. Patient Eligibility Criteria

4.1 Inclusion Criteria

  • Male or female, age 18 to 75 years, inclusive
  • Diagnosis of Type 2 Diabetes for at least 6 months prior to screening
  • HbA1c ≥7.0% and ≤10.0% at screening
  • Body Mass Index (BMI) ≥23 kg/m² and ≤40 kg/m² at screening

4.2 Exclusion Criteria

  • Diagnosis of type 1 diabetes mellitus
  • History of diabetic ketoacidosis
  • Treatment with insulin within 3 months prior to screening
  • Significant renal, hepatic, or cardiovascular disease

5. Treatment Administration

Patients will be randomized 1:1 to receive either the investigational product or placebo once daily for 24 weeks.

6. Safety Monitoring

Safety assessments will include adverse event monitoring, physical examinations, vital signs, ECGs, and laboratory assessments.

7. Statistical Analysis Plan

A sample size of 300 patients (150 per treatment arm) will provide 90% power to detect a treatment difference of 0.5% in HbA1c at Week 24 with a two-sided alpha of 0.05.
