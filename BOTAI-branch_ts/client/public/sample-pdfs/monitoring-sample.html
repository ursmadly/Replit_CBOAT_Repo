Monitoring Plan

Protocol: PRO001

Version: 1.1

Date: February 5, 2025

1. Introduction

This monitoring plan outlines the strategy and procedures for monitoring the PRO001 clinical trial. It incorporates risk-based monitoring principles to optimize resource allocation while ensuring data quality, patient safety, and protocol compliance.

Note: This plan should be implemented in conjunction with the study protocol, the Clinical Monitoring SOPs, and applicable regulatory requirements.

2. Risk Assessment

A comprehensive risk assessment has been conducted to identify critical data and processes. The following critical risk categories have been identified:

2.1 Critical Data and Processes

  • Primary efficacy endpoint: HbA1c measurements
  • Key safety parameters: hypoglycemic events, adverse events
  • Protocol compliance: inclusion/exclusion criteria, dosing adherence
  • Informed consent process
  • Investigational product accountability

2.2 Risk Mitigation Strategies

Risk Category Risk Level Mitigation Strategy Monitoring Approach
HbA1c Data Integrity High Centralized lab testing, standardized collection procedures 100% SDV for primary endpoint, central data review
Safety Reporting High Investigator training, automated safety alerts 100% SDV for AEs, remote review of safety trends
Eligibility Criteria Medium Pre-screening checklists, remote eligibility review 100% SDV for eligibility criteria, targeted site visits
Dosing Compliance Medium Patient diaries, drug accountability logs Targeted SDV, central compliance monitoring
General Data Quality Low eCRF design, edit checks, site training Reduced SDV (30%), central data monitoring

3. Monitoring Strategy

A hybrid monitoring approach will be implemented, combining on-site visits with remote/centralized monitoring. The frequency and depth of monitoring activities will be tailored to the site risk level.

3.1 Site Risk Categorization

Sites will be classified into three risk categories based on enrollment, data quality metrics, protocol compliance, and findings from previous monitoring visits:

  • Low Risk: Minimal findings, good compliance history, experienced staff
  • Medium Risk: Moderate findings, some quality issues, average experience
  • High Risk: Significant findings, quality concerns, less experienced staff

3.2 Monitoring Schedule

4. Source Data Verification (SDV)

A risk-based approach to SDV will be applied, with different verification rates for different data categories:

4.1 SDV Rates by Data Category

  • Critical data (100% SDV): Informed consent documentation, eligibility criteria, primary endpoint data, serious adverse events, study drug administration
  • Important data (50% SDV): Secondary endpoints, non-serious adverse events, concomitant medications
  • Non-critical data (30% SDV): Demographics, medical history, routine vital signs

4.2 SDV Adjustment

SDV rates may be adjusted based on site performance and data quality. Increases in SDV will be implemented if issues are identified during monitoring.

5. Centralized Monitoring

Centralized monitoring will be conducted to review data across sites, identify trends, and detect potential issues. The following analyses will be performed:

5.1 Key Performance Indicators

  • Enrollment rate vs. target
  • Screen failure rate
  • Protocol deviation rate
  • Query rate and resolution time
  • Missing data rate
  • Adverse event reporting rate

5.2 Statistical Monitoring

  • Between-site variability in key endpoints
  • Outlier detection for efficacy and safety parameters
  • Data patterns suggesting potential fabrication or errors

6. Issue Management and Escalation

Issues identified during monitoring will be documented, tracked, and resolved according to the following process:

6.1 Issue Classification

  • Critical: Patient safety concerns, serious GCP violations, fraud
  • Major: Protocol deviations affecting primary endpoints, systematic errors
  • Minor: Documentation issues, isolated errors

6.2 Escalation Pathway

  • Critical issues: Immediate escalation to Medical Monitor, Study Manager, and QA within 24 hours
  • Major issues: Escalation to Study Manager within 3 days
  • Minor issues: Addressed by CRA, documented in monitoring reports

7. Monitoring Reports and Documentation

All monitoring activities will be documented according to the following schedule:

  • On-site visit reports: Completed within 5 business days of visit completion
  • Remote monitoring reports: Completed weekly
  • Central monitoring reports: Generated monthly
  • Follow-up letters to sites: Sent within 10 business days of visit
